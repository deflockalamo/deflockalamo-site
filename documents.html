City of Alamogordo · 4 May 2026

Budget Hearing Workshop Minutes (DRAFT)

This is the most useful document on this page. It contains the only substantive public discussion of the Flock contract that we have found to date, including the contract value (~$250,000/yr), the camera count (50–60), the funding split ($100K from opioid funds, ~$150K from the General Fund), and a direct exchange between Commissioner Rardin and Police Chief Kunihiro about the cost and impact of the system.

City Commission minutes (Flock-related)

The City of Alamogordo often posts the same meeting minutes under multiple filenames — an early DRAFT (posted a few days after the meeting), and later a FINAL version (approved at the next meeting, sometimes with different file sizes and dates). We host both where we have them so visitors comparing against the city's own pages can find the file they expect. See the City's official minutes archive (CivicClerk) for the canonical versions.

  • 5 May 2026 — Budget Hearing Workshop (DRAFT)
    Contains the Flock contract discussion. · PDF · DRAFT only — the city has not yet posted a FINAL version for this meeting.
  • 6 May 2025 — Budget Hearing Workshop
    First public mention of Flock implementation progress ("46% live"). · DRAFT (PDF) · FINAL (PDF)
    Note: two files, different sizes (117 KB DRAFT vs 983 KB FINAL) — the city re-posted this meeting under two filenames. Both are the same meeting, the FINAL is the approved version with the full text.
  • 3 December 2024 — Regular Meeting
    Pre-dates the Flock discussion. Useful for context on the Commission's procedures. · DRAFT (PDF) · FINAL (PDF)
    Note: two files, very different sizes (192 KB DRAFT vs 1.6 MB FINAL) — the FINAL is the much longer approved version.

How we source documents

All documents on this page are either:

  • Public records obtained directly from the City of Alamogordo (e.g. via IPRA, public comment at meetings, or the city's own website).
  • Publicly available documents hosted by a government agency (e.g. nmlegis.gov).

We do not publish documents obtained through private channels, leaked information, or anything that would identify a private individual.
