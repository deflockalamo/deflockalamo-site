Resources

Primary sources, journalism, and explainers — both the case for ALPRs and the case against them. We link out rather than republish, and we try to summarize why each source is worth your time.

In favor of ALPRs

The Alamogordo Police Department has publicly stated that ALPRs are intended to support investigations of property and violent crime. The May 2026 budget workshop included a recent press release about an arrest assisted by ALPR data. Below is what the public record shows about how the system is being used and paid for.

City of Alamogordo · 4 May 2026 (Budget Workshop Minutes)

Flock Safety implementation update from Police Chief Kunihiro

cityofalamogordo.us · PDF on this site

Quoted at the budget workshop: "We put out a press release about an arrest last week. They absolutely have an impact." The chief also confirmed that the system is being rolled out citywide, with agreements with DOT and PNM being the slow point.

Read the minutes (PDF)

Concerns & criticism

Civil liberties organizations and several city governments have raised concerns about ALPRs generally, and about Flock specifically. We've grouped them so you can read across the same concerns in multiple sources.

EFF · 2 Jan 2026

EFF: Investigations Expose Flock Safety's Surveillance Abuses — 2025 in Review

eff.org

Year-end summary of EFF's investigations. Argues that Flock's announced "privacy enhancements" (geofencing, retention limits) don't make ALPRs safe, and documents specific cases of misuse.

Why some cities are canceling Flock license plate reader contracts

Overview of municipal cancellations in Hillsborough NC, Flagstaff AZ, and elsewhere, focusing on the "good faith belief" disclosure language in Flock's standard contract.
