What's happening

Automatic License Plate Readers (ALPRs) made by Flock Safety are being installed in our area. Each camera logs every passing vehicle's license plate, time, location, and direction of travel — often sharing that data with a nationwide network of agencies and, in some cases, federal partners.

This site is a community-run information hub. It is not affiliated with any local government, police department, or vendor. Our goal is simple: help residents understand what's deployed, why it matters, and how to make their voices heard.

Why this matters

Privacy isn't a partisan issue. ALPRs create a permanent log of where everyone drives — to work, school, medical appointments, places of worship, and to protest. Once the data exists, it can be queried, shared, and abused.
  • Mass surveillance, not targeted policing. These cameras record every vehicle, not just suspected ones.
  • Data sharing is broad. Many Flock deployments allow searches by out-of-area and federal agencies, sometimes with minimal oversight.
  • Public records have been fought for in court. A Washington state court recently ruled that Flock-captured data are public records — and agencies have been shown to resist disclosure.
  • Cancellation is hard. Reports describe cities stuck in "contractual trap" arrangements where cameras keep running long after a vote to end the program.
