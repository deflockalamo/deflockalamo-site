Video education

Business Reform is a YouTube channel that covers Flock and other private surveillance companies in depth. We feature their videos here because they explain the business model, the contracts, and the data-sharing issues better than almost anything else out there. Start with the one below — it's the one that flipped how a lot of people think about "their" cameras.

Featured · Business Reform

Public Officials Fear This: How YOU Can Use Surveillance Data Against Them

The punchy one. Turns the script on the "if you have nothing to hide, you have nothing to fear" argument: the same camera data can be used as evidence against the officials who deploy it. Great share video.

Business Reform

Sanibel Police Refuse Open Records Request on Flock Safety Cameras

A real case study in what happens when a community asks its police department, in writing, what it signed with Flock. Sanibel said no. Useful precedent if you ever file a public-records request here.

National coverage & explainers

NPR · 17 Feb 2026

Why some cities are canceling Flock license plate reader contracts

npr.org

Good overview of why municipal leaders — including Hillsborough, NC and Flagstaff, AZ — have ended their Flock contracts, with focus on data-sharing language and "good faith belief" disclosure clauses.

EFF · 2 Jan 2026

EFF's Investigations Expose Flock Safety's Surveillance Abuses: 2025 in Review

eff.org

EFF's annual review of their Flock investigations. Covers why the company's "privacy enhancements" (geofencing, retention limits) don't make ALPRs safe.

ACLU · 17 Oct 2025

Flock's Aggressive Expansions Go Far Beyond Simple Driver Surveillance

aclu.org

ACLU's "Flock roundup" — collection of cases and commentary, including the Nguyen v. State case examining whether ALPRs violate Fourth Amendment expectations of privacy.

Berkeleyside · 24 Nov 2025

What are Flock cameras, and why are they controversial in Berkeley?

berkeleyside.org

Accessible explainer for a general audience, with quotes from ACLU and EFF and a clear breakdown of the constitutional concerns.

Fortune · 3 Jun 2026

Dayton is covering Flock cameras with trash bags after officials found data use violated policy

fortune.com

Concrete example of a city discovering after the fact that data was being shared in ways the contract didn't allow — and the "contractual trap" problem with cancellation.

EFF · 12 Nov 2025

Washington Court Rules That Data Captured on Flock Safety Cameras Are Public Records

eff.org

Important precedent: a Washington court ruled that Flock-captured data are public records, reinforcing transparency obligations. Useful for FOIA arguments locally.
